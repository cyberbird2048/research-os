# <Task ID> Source Register

Every source cited in the evidence ledger or historical draft must have an entry here.

## Register

| Source | Author | Date | Source Type | Primary/Secondary | Claims Supported | Reliability | Access Path |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Example: "Railways and British Economic Growth 1840-1870" | G.R. Hawke | 1970 | academic monograph | secondary | CLM-001, CLM-004 | high | library / institutional access |

## Priority Order

Per `governance/HIS-001_RESEARCH_BRIEF.md` §6:

1. Primary historical data
2. Company accounts, security prices, dividends, and failure records
3. Official statistics
4. Academic economic history
5. High-quality historical synthesis
6. Popular books (orientation only, not evidentiary support)

## Rules

- A popular-press source may motivate a question; it cannot alone support a claim classified as historical fact or quantitative estimate.
- Reliability is a judgment on the source itself (methodology, bias, distance from primary data), not on whether it supports the desired conclusion.
- Sources presenting competing interpretations must be flagged in `Claims Supported` even when they contradict other rows.
