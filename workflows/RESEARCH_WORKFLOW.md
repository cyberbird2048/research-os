# Research Workflow

Canonical execution sequence for Phase 2 historical research tasks (e.g. `HIS-001`). Governs the chain: historical evidence → economic mechanism → reusable pattern → AI-era mapping. It does not authorize thesis creation; see `workflows/THESIS_WORKFLOW.md`.

## Step 1 — Repository Intake

Before any task work, read in order:

1. `README.md`
2. `governance/PROJECT_CONSTITUTION.md`
3. `governance/RESEARCH_SCOPE.md`
4. `governance/OPERATING_PRINCIPLES.md`
5. `governance/NAMING_AND_IDS.md`
6. `governance/FRONTMATTER_STANDARD.md`
7. `governance/AGENTS.md`
8. `governance/WORK_BOARD.md`
9. `workflows/RESEARCH_WORKFLOW.md` (this file)
10. the active research brief (e.g. `governance/HIS-001_RESEARCH_BRIEF.md`)

Do not skip or reorder this list, and do not begin research on the strength of the brief alone.

## Step 2 — Claim the Task

Claim the task in `governance/WORK_BOARD.md` before doing any research work. Only one historical research task is open at a time.

## Step 3 — Research Execution Plan, Then Stop

Produce a Research Execution Plan only: question tree, evidence needs per question, source strategy, file plan, known risks and biases, stopping conditions, unresolved design questions. Do not draft conclusions or narrative. Stop and submit for **Gate 1 — Research Design Review**.

**Gate 1 pass criteria:** one primary question; bounded scope; explicit evidence requirements; explicit counter-evidence; no premature AI conclusion.

## Step 4 — Evidence Ledger Before Narrative

Build the evidence ledger (`templates/EVIDENCE_LEDGER_TEMPLATE.md`) before writing any narrative. Narrative begins only once evidence exists across all categories required by the brief. Submit for **Gate 2 — Evidence Sufficiency Review**.

**Gate 2 pass criteria:** investor-return evidence included; failure cases included; competing interpretations included; operating profit separated from shareholder return.

## Step 5 — Historical Draft

Draft the historical research asset. No AI investment claims inside historical files. Every major claim is classified as historical fact, quantitative estimate, scholarly interpretation, or research inference. Submit with explicit gaps (weak claims, missing data, unresolved debates, possible survivorship bias, unsupported analogies) for **Gate 3 — Historical Stability / gap review**.

**Gate 3 pass criteria:** claims are traceable; uncertainties are explicit; no major unsupported generalization.

## Step 6 — Pattern Promotion

Promote only recurring, mechanism-based findings to pattern assets (`templates/PATTERN_TEMPLATE.md`). Never promote a pattern to `active` from one anecdote; promotion to `active` requires at least two independent historical instances.

## Step 7 — Historical Mapping

Create the AI-era mapping only after historical claims are stable enough for comparison. The mapping must include a mandatory critical-differences section; it cannot be submitted empty. Submit for **Gate 4 — Mapping Quality Review**.

**Gate 4 pass criteria:** shared mechanism identified; critical differences documented; analogy is falsifiable; AI implications are narrower than historical evidence.

## Gate 5 — Thesis Eligibility

A thesis may be created only after the mapping passes Gate 4, and only if: historical mechanism is credible; current evidence exists; investable universe is defined; falsification metrics are available. Thesis creation is additionally gated by the Phase 3 entry conditions in `governance/RESEARCH_SCOPE.md`; see `workflows/THESIS_WORKFLOW.md`.

## Stopping Conditions

End the sprint when the historical mechanism is sufficiently clear, major scholarly disputes are documented, investor-return evidence is adequate, candidate patterns are supported or rejected, the AI analogy has explicit boundaries, and remaining gaps are non-critical. Do not continue merely to accumulate more sources.
