# Research Scope

## Current Scope

The research scope is intentionally narrow and sequential:

1. Understand how previous technological revolutions created and redistributed wealth.
2. Map recurring patterns from those revolutions to the AI revolution.
3. Convert the strongest mappings into testable investment theses.
4. Review and update those theses through evidence.

No step may be skipped or reordered. Step 3 (theses) does not begin until step 1 and step 2 have produced sufficient validated material (see entry gates below).

## Phase Model

### Phase 1 — Foundation

Status: **complete**.

Established the governance layer, naming and frontmatter standards, agent roles, work board, decision log, and canonical templates. No research content was produced in Phase 1.

### Phase 2 — Historical Research

Status: **active**.

Single authorized task: `HIS-001 — Steam, Railways, and Wealth Creation in the First Industrial Revolution`.

Rules:

- Only one historical research task is open at a time.
- No second historical revolution research task begins until HIS-001 is either stable and reviewed, or explicitly closed.
- The objective is not to write industrial revolution history. It is to answer: which mechanisms of wealth creation, capture, and destruction during the steam and railway era are legitimately reusable for analyzing the AI infrastructure cycle.

### Phase 3 — Investment Thesis Engine

Status: **locked**. Must not start early, regardless of apparent readiness of any single asset.

Entry gates (all required):

- At least 3 completed Historical Revolution Research assets (`REV-HIST-###`)
- At least 5 cross-cycle validated Patterns (`PAT-###`)
- At least 3 Historical Mappings (`MAP-###`)
- At least 1 completed Monthly Review (`MR-YYYY-MM`)
- A stable, repeatable Phase 2 workflow (evidenced by having run it more than once without redesign)

No thesis (`THS-###`) is created before these gates are satisfied, except as an explicitly labeled candidate pending its own mapping's review (see `governance/HIS-001_RESEARCH_BRIEF.md` §9F).

## Explicit Out of Scope

The following are out of scope until the relevant phase gate opens:

- Parallel research into multiple technological revolutions at the same time.
- Any automation or ingestion pipeline (news feeds, scrapers, scheduled jobs, agents that write without review).
- Portfolio recommendations, position sizing, or trade execution guidance.
- Premature investment theses created before historical mechanisms are evidenced and reviewed.
- New governance modules, roles, or file categories that are not required by the current phase.

## Changing Scope

Scope may only be changed by an explicit decision recorded in `governance/DECISIONS.md`. Agents must not silently broaden scope while executing a task.
